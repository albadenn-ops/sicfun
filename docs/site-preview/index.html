What SICFUN Is

It is software for Texas Hold'em analysis, simulation, and decision review.

SICFUN is not a generic "AI poker thing." It is a Texas Hold'em study engine built for replaying hands, testing strategy assumptions, simulating play, and inspecting the reasoning behind a result.

The point is simple: the name can wink a little, but the work still has to hold up.

Start Here

If you are new, here is the simple version.

SICFUN is a poker analysis engine built around one idea: good poker study should be testable. Instead of just giving you answers, it helps you inspect decisions, compare alternatives, and understand why a line makes sense.

The promise is not "trust the machine." The promise is "look at the evidence."

Why Players Care

Why would a poker player actually need this?

Expose the leak

See where your EV is really disappearing.

Hand study is usually too fuzzy. SICFUN is meant to help isolate the costly decisions so your review time goes to the spots that matter.

Test the idea

Stop arguing about lines you have never tested.

If you think a sizing, range assumption, or exploit works, the right response is to test it in simulation, not just defend it in conversation.

Trust the reasoning

Get analysis you can inspect, not outputs you have to worship.

SICFUN is strongest when it shows its work: the event path, the model used, the signals produced, and the assumptions behind the result.

What You Get

What a newcomer should expect from SICFUN in practice.

Hand review

Actual action vs recommended action.

You should be able to look at a hand, see the decision taken, compare it to a stronger line, and estimate what that mistake cost.

Signals

Risk flags with model and event trace.

The system should tell you why a spot is dangerous and what model or assumptions were involved in producing that warning.

Simulation

Controlled runs for testing strategy ideas.

If you want to know whether a line is worth building around, simulate it, compare outcomes, and inspect the result instead of trusting instinct.
