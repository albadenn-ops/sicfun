The Problem

Most poker study fails in the same three places.

You replay hands from memory.

Memory edits the parts that hurt, which makes honest review harder than it should be.

You argue lines with no data.

Everyone sounds confident until someone has to produce a method and a number.

You fix a leak and reopen it later.

Without disciplined review, the same expensive mistake keeps finding its way back.

How It Works

Replay. Compare. Test.

01

Replay the hand

Bring the action, position, and sizing back into view without post-session fog.

02

Compare decisions

Put your action next to the recommended line and see what the gap costs in expected value.

03

Test the better line

If you think a different line holds up, simulate it and get evidence instead of table talk.
